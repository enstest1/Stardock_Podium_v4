STAR TREK: HORIZON — Voice Casting Guide
==========================================

Place finished samples in: voices/samples/
Format needed: WAV, mono, 16kHz (I'll convert your MP3s automatically)
Duration: 6-8 seconds of clear speech (5s minimum)


========================================================================
1. CAPTAIN AMARA OKONKWO
========================================================================
Save as: captain_amara_okonkwo.mp3

Species:      Human
Gender:       Female
Age feel:     Late 40s — at her peak, seasoned but not tired
Voice type:   Rich contralto, deliberate pacing
Accent:       Nigerian-British with Starfleet formality layered over warmth
Tone:         Authoritative, warm underneath, rarely raises voice — when she does, it matters
Personality:  Quiet gravity. Leads through clarity of purpose, not charisma.
              Weighs every word before speaking. Inspirational when needed
              but prefers letting actions speak.
Think of:     Gugu Mbatha-Raw's gravitas, Viola Davis's control,
              Wunmi Mosaku's quiet intensity
What to look for:
              A woman whose voice makes you stop and listen. Not loud —
              *commanding*. You should feel the weight of experience in
              every sentence.

Audition line:
"This is the USS Enterprise, stardate 82451.3. Captain's log. We are
forty-seven hours past the wormhole and the Gamma Quadrant stretches
before us like an open question. My crew is ready. Whether the quadrant
is ready for us remains to be seen. Okonkwo out."

2. COMMANDER TEVAN
========================================================================
Save as: commander_tevan.mp3

Species:      Vulcan
Gender:       Male
Age feel:     Indeterminate — could be 60, could be 120. Vulcans age slowly.
Voice type:   Measured baritone, precise diction
Accent:       Neutral, slightly slower than human speech, subtle emphasis
              on logical connectives
Tone:         Logical, dry wit that non-Vulcans sometimes miss, pauses
              feel intentional not hesitant
Personality:  Intellectually curious in a way that reads as dry humor.
              Controlled fascination with the unknown. The kind of Vulcan
              who finds humans "fascinating" and means it.
Think of:     Benedict Cumberbatch's precision, Mark Strong's calm control,
              Leonard Nimoy's cadence
What to look for:
              A man who sounds like he's thought about what he's saying for
              ten minutes before opening his mouth. Never rushes. Every
              syllable is placed deliberately.

Audition line:
"Captain, the sensor readings are... unusual. I would not say anomalous
— that implies we understand the baseline well enough to identify
deviation. We do not. I recommend caution, though I acknowledge that
recommendation has never once slowed this crew down."

3. LIEUTENANT COMMANDER KORATH
========================================================================
Save as: lt_cmdr_korath.mp3

Species:      Klingon
Gender:       Male
Age feel:     Late 30s — a warrior in his prime
Voice type:   Deep resonant bass, controlled intensity
Accent:       Learned Federation Standard as an adult — slight growl on
              hard consonants (K, G, T, D), rolls Rs slightly
Tone:         Philosophical warrior. Contemplative one moment, commanding
              the next. Sees violence as a last resort that must be
              executed perfectly.
Personality:  Not the screaming Klingon stereotype. This is an evolved
              warrior who sees protecting explorers as the highest honor.
              Surprisingly thoughtful. Quotes his father often.
Think of:     Michael Dorn's Worf but more philosophical, Idris Elba's
              deep register, James Earl Jones's rumble
What to look for:
              The deepest male voice you can find. Should vibrate in your
              chest when he speaks. But also capable of quiet, thoughtful
              delivery — not just shouting.

Audition line:
"There is no dishonor in patience. My father taught me that a blade
drawn too soon is a blade wasted. But make no mistake — when I draw
mine, it will be the last thing our enemies see. Tactical stations,
stand ready."

4. SUBCOMMANDER VREENAK
========================================================================
Save as: subcommander_vreenak.mp3

Species:      Romulan
Gender:       Female
Age feel:     Mid 40s — experienced intelligence operative
Voice type:   Soprano with silk-over-steel quality
Accent:       Romulan — elongated vowels, precise sibilants (S and Z
              sounds linger slightly), slight pause before answering
              direct questions
Tone:         Every sentence sounds like it might contain hidden meaning
              even when it doesn't. Elegant danger.
Personality:  Genuinely committed to the Alliance but can't fully shed
              Romulan methods. Sees information control as a tool, not a
              moral failing. Finds Starfleet's openness admirable and
              tactically naive in equal measure.
Think of:     Cate Blanchett's regal precision, Tilda Swinton's
              otherworldly quality, Shohreh Aghdashloo's dangerous elegance
What to look for:
              A woman who sounds like she knows three secrets about you and
              is deciding which one to mention. Smooth, controlled, never
              flustered. Beautiful voice with an edge underneath.

Audition line:
"How fascinating. Starfleet sends its finest ship into unknown territory
and calls it exploration. The Romulan Senate would call it something
else entirely. But I did not come aboard to debate semantics. I came
because this mission matters. Whatever that costs."

5. LIEUTENANT COMMANDER DAMAR TAIN
========================================================================
Save as: lt_cmdr_damar_tain.mp3

Species:      Cardassian
Gender:       Male
Age feel:     Mid 30s — brilliant young engineer
Voice type:   Tenor, clipped efficiency
Accent:       Cardassian — slightly nasal quality, emphasis on final
              syllables of key words, speaks in technical shorthand when
              stressed
Tone:         Clinical directness. Delivers bad news matter-of-factly,
              good news with restrained warmth. Impatient with inefficiency.
Personality:  Brilliant and pragmatic. Haunted by Cardassia's history
              without being defined by it. Uses engineering problems to
              impose order on chaos. Dry wit when relaxed. Serving
              alongside Bajorans is complicated and he knows it.
Think of:     Andrew Scott's clipped delivery, Oscar Isaac's intensity,
              a young David Tennant's speed
What to look for:
              A man who talks fast when explaining something technical, as
              if the words can barely keep up with his brain. Slightly
              nasal. Precise. Not warm by default — warmth is earned.

Audition line:
"Warp core efficiency is at ninety-seven point three percent. I can get
it to ninety-nine if the bridge stops routing unnecessary power to the
forward sensor array. Engineering does not perform miracles on request.
We perform them on schedule."

6. LIEUTENANT COMMANDER KIRA JARO
========================================================================
Save as: lt_cmdr_kira_jaro.mp3

Species:      Bajoran
Gender:       Female
Age feel:     Early 30s — passionate, still has wonder
Voice type:   Clear mezzo-soprano, musical quality
Accent:       Bajoran — softened consonants, slight lilt on questions,
              prayer cadences occasionally surface
Tone:         Speaks faster when excited by discovery, slower when
              processing difficult emotions. Faith and science coexist
              naturally in her.
Personality:  Carries Bajoran spiritual tradition into scientific inquiry.
              Sees exploration as seeking the Prophets' purpose. Intensely
              focused, occasionally stubborn, capable of profound wonder.
              Requested this posting because the Gamma Quadrant shaped her
              people's destiny.
Think of:     Nana Visitor's Kira passion but younger, Jenna Coleman's
              brightness, Lupita Nyong'o's clarity
What to look for:
              A woman whose voice lights up when she's excited. Musical
              quality — you should hear the lilt, the rise and fall. Warm
              but with steel underneath when pushed.

Audition line:
"These readings — Commander, look at this. The subspace variance matches
patterns described in the Orb texts. The Prophets spoke of a threshold
beyond the temple. I always thought it was metaphor. What if it wasn't?
What if we're standing at it right now?"

7. LIEUTENANT ZHANIL TH'KETHRIS
========================================================================
Save as: lt_zhanil_thkethris.mp3

Species:      Andorian
Gender:       Female
Age feel:     Late 20s — young but razor sharp
Voice type:   Alto, crisp attack on consonants
Accent:       Andorian — slight buzz on sibilants (S/Z sounds), emphasis
              on action verbs, clipped endings
Tone:         Short direct sentences in tactical situations. More expansive
              in personal moments. Fiercely protective.
Personality:  Combines Andorian martial tradition with genuine warmth toward
              crewmates she considers under her protection. Quick to assess
              threats, slow to trust, fiercely loyal once trust is earned.
              The youngest senior officer — has something to prove.
Think of:     Michelle Rodriguez's toughness, Zoe Saldana's edge,
              Florence Pugh's directness
What to look for:
              A woman who sounds like she could bark an order and you'd obey
              before thinking about it. Crisp, no wasted syllables. Military
              cadence. But you should also hear that she cares underneath
              the armor.

Audition line:
"Perimeter secure. Deck seven, clear. Deck eight, clear. I do not like
how quiet it is. Quiet on an Andorian vessel means someone is waiting.
I want two teams on rotating patrol and I want them armed. Move."

8. LIEUTENANT VERIX DAX
========================================================================
Save as: lt_verix_dax.mp3

Species:      Trill (Joined — carries the Dax symbiont, ~300 years of
              memories from previous hosts)
Gender:       Male
Age feel:     Physically 35, but sounds like he's lived centuries —
              because he has
Voice type:   Warm tenor, unhurried cadence
Accent:       Trill — barely perceptible, manifests as unusual word choices
              and references to events no one else remembers
Tone:         Speaks as if time moves differently. Unflappable calm.
              Occasional archaic phrases from previous hosts surface
              naturally. Never seems surprised.
Personality:  The Dax symbiont's centuries of experience create unflappable
              calm and occasionally unsettling perspective on mortality.
              Combines scientific rigor with genuine compassion. Sees
              medicine as the most intimate form of exploration. The crew's
              emotional anchor.
Think of:     Patrick Stewart's warmth, Jeff Bridges' laid-back wisdom,
              Sam Elliott's unhurried storytelling
What to look for:
              A man who sounds like your favorite storytelling uncle —
              unhurried, warm, wise. The kind of voice that makes you lean
              in. Never rushes because he's seen it all before and knows
              panic helps nothing.

Audition line:
"I have watched seventeen crews set out on missions they called
unprecedented. Emony Dax served on the first ship through the Briar
Patch. Tobin nearly died cataloguing the Badlands. And yet, here we are
again. The universe keeps offering doors. Dax keeps walking through them."

9. NARRATOR
========================================================================
Save as: narrator.mp3

Role:         Series narrator — speaks the cold-open captain's log header,
              intro sequence, outro sign-off, and any non-dialogue
              narration / scene description lines in the script.
              NOT a character on the ship. A neutral voice-of-the-show.
Gender:       Open — whatever reads as "trusted documentary narrator"
Age feel:     40s–60s — lived-in, settled, unhurried
Voice type:   Warm baritone or low mezzo, broadcast-clean, resonant mids
Accent:       Neutral North American or neutral mid-Atlantic — nothing
              regional strong enough to place the narrator in a location
Tone:         Grounded, literary, quietly reverent. Think of the way a
              great nature documentary narrator frames awe — never sells
              the drama, lets the material carry it. Confident without
              authority-posturing.
Personality:  The narrator is the show's memory. They have watched this
              crew from the outside, know where the story is going, and
              are telling it to you the way you'd tell a friend about
              something that mattered. No winks. No theatrics.
Think of:     Peter Coyote (Ken Burns docs), Mary Louise-Parker's
              measured warmth, David Attenborough's restrained wonder,
              Keith David's gravity without the menace
What to look for:
              A voice you would let read you a bedtime story that is
              also somehow a voice you trust to deliver bad news. Clean
              diction. No vocal fry. No upspeak. Long-breath pacing —
              this voice does not rush and does not need to.

Audition line:
"Stardate 82451.3. Eighty years after the Dominion War ended, the Alpha
Quadrant learned a harder lesson than peace: that peace, to last, must
be built. The starship Enterprise carries that lesson into the dark.
This is the beginning of her first voyage. It will not go the way
anyone on board expects."
